# Ideas Backlog

These are candidates, not committed requirements. Promote an idea only after defining its user value, source requirements, failure modes, and acceptance criteria.

## High-value candidates

### Tank Race

A league view focused on teams with the highest scores and how the rankings changed this week.

Why it may work: highly shareable and naturally recurring.  
Risk: can reward sensationalism; explanations must remain prominent.

### Bad or Tanking? quadrant

Plot team strength against competitive intent. This directly communicates that weak teams can still be trying.

Why it may work: expresses the product thesis better than a one-dimensional ranking.  
Dependency: stable team-strength and intent scores.

### Draft Prize meter

Show how attractive the upcoming class is and which prospect tiers are driving the incentive.

Potential fields:

- Franchise-prospect tier.
- Top-five depth.
- Lottery depth.
- Consensus change.
- Confidence.

### Pick incentive map

Show whether each team owns, owes, swaps, or protects its pick and who benefits from its losses.

Why it may work: this is often the missing explanation in casual tanking discussion.  
Risk: protection chains are complex and easy to display incorrectly.

### Evidence timeline

Combine transactions, availability, rotations, official action, reporting, and score changes into a chronological story.

### Social Heat

Separate fan-discussion volume from Tank Score.

Possible display:

- “Quiet,” “Rising,” “Hot,” “League-wide debate.”
- Most discussed team this week.
- Links to representative public discussions.

Guardrail: engagement cannot establish intent or medical availability.

### This Week in Tanking

An automatically drafted, manually reviewed weekly article:

- Biggest score increases/decreases.
- New evidence.
- Draft-class changes.
- Notable fan debates.
- Corrections or methodology updates.

### Tanking case files

Historical explainers for prominent cases, starting with Utah and Indiana in 2025-26. Useful for methodology validation and search traffic.

## Medium-priority candidates

- Side-by-side team comparison.
- “Why did this score change?” diff view.
- Score component tooltips with raw facts.
- User-selectable time window: 7, 14, or 30 days.
- Trade deadline before/after view.
- Mathematical elimination tracker.
- Remaining schedule difficulty.
- Available talent versus deployed talent.
- Expected wins versus actual wins.
- Closing-spread performance from YunoBall.
- Young-player minutes share.
- Veteran minutes decline.
- Star participation tracker.
- Team-specific RSS/JSON feeds.
- Weekly email digest.
- Downloadable CSV/JSON historical snapshots.

## Experimental candidates

- A classifier that suggests evidence components from articles.
- Transcript scanning for coach/GM language.
- Community predictions displayed as a separate poll.
- Automated social share images.
- “Tank move of the night” with mandatory human review.
- Draft-pick outcome simulator under the 3-2-1 Lottery.
- Counterfactual score: “What if this team controlled its pick?”
- API for embedding team cards elsewhere.

## Ideas explicitly deferred

- Anonymous public comments.
- User-submitted accusations without moderation.
- Automated medical skepticism.
- Gambling or betting picks.
- Scraping private or authenticated communities.
- Automatically quoting large portions of copyrighted articles.
- Directly copying NBA/team visual identity as the site's brand.

## Content and distribution ideas

- Weekly Reddit post built around the methodology and changes, not link dumping.
- Team-sub comparisons after a meaningful score movement.
- Short-form “Bad or Tanking?” explainers.
- Draft-class strength update when consensus materially shifts.
- Methodology/backtest post for technically minded fans.
- End-of-season review comparing Tank Score with lottery outcomes and NBA enforcement.

## Idea promotion template

Before promoting an item, document:

- User problem.
- Proposed behavior.
- Required data and source rights.
- Trust/safety risk.
- Success metric.
- Engineering estimate.
- Dependencies.
- Acceptance criteria.
